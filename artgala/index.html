Celebrating Art & Community

The Rutgers MSA Art Gala brought together talented artists and community members to celebrate creativity while supporting meaningful causes. Through the sale of beautiful artworks, we raised funds to support organizations making a difference in our communities and around the world.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who Can Attend Art Gala?
Art Gala is open for anyone to donate a piece, bid on art, and attend! Rutgers' students, alumni, and families are welcome. All attendees are required to purchase a ticket to enter. Additional information on where and how to purchase a ticket will be made available on our Instagram soon.
How Can I Bid on a Piece?
Art Gala is run auction-style. You must bid on a piece and compete with other attendees for it. All bids will happen in-person.
What Can I Submit?
We accept all types of art like canvas paintings, prints, textile pieces (like clothing, crochet or embroidery), pottery, sculpture, and even services like baked goods and photography sessions. Make sure your submissions align with Islamic values!
Where Can I Submit my Pieces?
Art collection will begin on October 16th and will be available weekly at MSA Thursdays and Rutgers Jumu'ah. Stay on the lookout for additional drop-off information on our story! If these drop-off timings are inconvenient for you, reach out to @rusubmissions or @rumsa.hope to arrange a different timing/location.

Beneficiary Organizations

Your support directly benefits these incredible organizations

La Cocina Comunitaria

La Cocina works to solve problems of equity in business ownership for women, immigrants, and people of color. They provide affordable kitchen space, hands-on training, mentorship, and market opportunities to help entrepreneurs formalize and grow their food businesses.

Palestine Children's Relief Fund

PCRF is a nonprofit organization founded in 1991 that provides free medical care to thousands of injured and ill children who lack local access to care within the local health care system. They have sent over 2,000 children abroad for free medical care and have established pediatric cancer departments in Palestine.
